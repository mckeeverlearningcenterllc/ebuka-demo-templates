Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the questions every Lagos property buyer asks

How do I verify that a property is not a scam before paying?
Always demand the original title document (C of O, Deed of Assignment, or Governor's Consent) and verify it independently at the Lagos State Land Registry before making any payment. A registered estate agent like Lagos Prime Realty conducts a full title search on every property we list. You should also visit the property in person (or with a trusted representative), confirm the seller's identity, and never pay cash without a formal receipt or agreement. If a deal seems too good to be true in Lagos real estate, it almost always is.
Can I buy property in Lagos if I live in the diaspora?
Yes, absolutely. Many of our clients are Nigerians based in the UK, USA, Canada, and Europe who purchase property remotely. The process typically involves: (1) identifying your preferred property via our listings or WhatsApp consultation, (2) virtual or video viewing, (3) instructing a local trusted representative via Power of Attorney, (4) our team conducting due diligence, and (5) completing documentation and payment remotely. We have a proven track record of handling diaspora purchases safely and efficiently.
How much is the agency fee in Lagos, and who pays it?
The standard agency fee in Lagos for rental properties is 10% of the annual rent, typically paid by the tenant. For property sales and purchases, agency commission is usually 5–10% of the property value, paid by the seller (though this can vary). We are fully transparent about our fees before any transaction begins — there are no hidden charges. We will always provide a written breakdown of all fees involved before you commit to anything.

We can connect you with Nigerian banks that offer property financing, as well as the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) for NHF-eligible buyers. We can also assist with the property valuation report that banks require for mortgage applications. Contact us to discuss your financing options.
How long does the Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) process take?
Processing a C of O in Lagos State typically takes between 3 to 12 months depending on the type of land, completeness of documents, and the current workload at the Lagos State Land Bureau. The process involves survey plan submission, application, charting, site inspection, assessment, and final issuance. Our documentation team (led by Yetunde Balogun) handles this process on behalf of clients and provides regular updates throughout. We also handle Governor's Consent and Deed of Assignment perfection.
What does property management include and how much does it cost?
Our property management service covers tenant sourcing and vetting, tenancy agreement drafting, rent collection, maintenance coordination and supervision, periodic property inspections, and tenant liaison. Our management fee is typically 8–10% of the annual rent collected, charged only when rent is received. There are no upfront management fees. This means you earn passive income from your property without the stress of day-to-day management. Contact us for a detailed proposal.
Do developers offer payment plans for property purchases in Lagos?
Yes, many property developers in Lagos offer flexible payment plans — especially for off-plan developments. Typical structures include an initial deposit (usually 20–30%) followed by installment payments over 12, 24, or 36 months. However, payment plans carry additional risks (e.g., developer delays), so it is essential to deal only with reputable, LASREA-registered developers. We work with vetted developers and will always advise you on the risks and protections available before you commit to an installment purchase.
What title documents are acceptable when buying property in Lagos?
In Lagos, acceptable title documents include: (1) Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) — the strongest form of title, issued by the Governor; (2) Governor's Consent — required when a property with a C of O changes hands; (3) Deed of Assignment — a transfer of ownership document, valid but less robust without accompanying perfection; (4) Registered Survey Plan — confirms the land boundaries. A Deed of Assignment alone, without Governor's Consent or a C of O in the buyer's name, carries higher risk. Always ensure your purchase is properly perfected at the Land Registry.
